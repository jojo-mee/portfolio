Hypothermia Infographic 1

Hypothermia Infographic 2

OVERVIEW:

CONTEXT

This infographic is designed to give the user basic knowledge about hypothermia. Image conbine with simple text help user learn new knowledge more easily and effectively way. This is made for new arrivals to northern mountain climate city, such as tourists, workers. At the same time, it can also serves the local people who are going to work in an environment with either artificial or natural cold. █

ROLL

Individual work
Graphic designer Graphic designer - Sketching & Adobe Illustrator

In order to finish this work, I sketch out ideas and create the final infographic in Adobe Illustrator. █

PROCESS:

STEP 1: Analyze Data

Comfirm the user. Collect and analyzed data.

Organize the required information into one document.

Select the target audience, the stakeholder, and the purpose of the production.

STEP 2: Brainstorm

Brainstorming to generate severl ideas and sketch them out to see the result.

Sketch out variaty of possibilities for each part, and combine them later.

Sketch out 4 ways for display clothing part.

CHALLENGE & SOLUTION:

For some part, it is hard to find the most effective way to perform. Therefore, I come up with several ideas for showing one specific part can help designer to chose the best option.

STEP 3: First Draft

Create the digital drafts based on sketches. Get feedback to see does the graphic help users understand the conten.

I get feedback saying the spacing is too crowded and some partâ€™s layout is a bit messy which makes it hard to read.

CHALLENGE & SOLUTION:

In design, there are two main ways (horizontal and vertical) to show a infographic poster, I need to find the best solution for my project. Therefore, I tried to create the infographic in both horizontal and vertical ways and ask for peer review.

STEP 4: Finalize

Refine the design based on the feedback.

I increaced the spacing to fix the readability problem from feedback.

From feedback vertical layout is easier to read, so I unify both design.

TAKEAWAYS:

Goal and Requirements Oriented

When designing, we first need to identify who the target audience is, which determines the form of the content and the purpose of the visualization. When dealing with people who have no knowledge of the subject, it is important to combine graphics and text as much as possible to help people understand what we are talking about. █

Designing and Desision

At the beginning of the design process, we should make good use of brainstorming to try out a wide range of ideas. Don't be afraid to give multiple possibilities to compare options and make improvements. It's easy to be stuck in a rut with one solution. The same is true in typography, where we can experiment with the process when we're not sure which will work better, landscape or portrait. A visual comparison or asking for feedback can help us make a better choice. █
